## Recommendations

### R1: 381-ticker universe generates too many marginal short-duration trades
**Category:** `params-tickers`
**Priority:** `high`
**Seen in:** `autoresearch/price-volume-updates`
**Source:** `harness_upgrade.md` + `trades.tsv analysis`
**Rationale:** Baseline ran 385 trades with avg P&L near zero ($0.22/trade net). 343 of 385 (89%) were held 1–5 days at a net −$120.97 loss. A large universe dilutes signal quality and floods the trade log with marginal breakouts that immediately reverse. The trades.tsv confirms the problem is concentrated in short-hold exits — consistent with low-conviction entries generated by weak signals in a diluted universe.
**Suggested change:** Reduce default universe to 100–150 high-quality liquid tickers (top 150 by 3-month avg dollar volume). Alternatively, add a minimum ATR-normalized dollar volume filter in `screen_day()` as a fast pre-check. A smaller universe should reduce daily trade count and improve entry quality, shifting the distribution toward 6–14d holds.

### R2: Trailing stop activation is the highest-leverage parameter — test it first
**Category:** `harness-objective`
**Priority:** `high`
**Seen in:** `autoresearch/price-volume-updates`
**Source:** `harness_upgrade.md`
**Rationale:** Changing trailing stop activation from 2.0 ATR to 1.5 ATR improved min_test_pnl by +$34.79 — the largest single gain of the entire 10-iteration run, dominating all screener changes combined. Yet program.md schedules position management experiments in iterations 6–10, causing this key discovery to be delayed.
**Suggested change:** In `program.md`, move position management experiments (trailing stop activation, breakeven trigger) to iterations 2–4. These interact with every screener setup and should be calibrated before screener tuning begins.

### R3: 1–5 day exits are value-destroying — investigate breakeven trigger at day 3
**Category:** `harness-structure`
**Priority:** `high`
**Seen in:** `autoresearch/price-volume-updates`
**Source:** `trades.tsv analysis`
**Rationale:** 343 trades (89%) were held 1–5 days with a 49% win rate and −$120.97 net. The remaining 40 trades held 6+ days had a 100% win rate and +$255.18 net. This is not a random split — it suggests the 1–5d exits are predominantly whipsaw stops: the stock triggers the pivot stop during early volatility, then continues upward after the position is closed. A breakeven trigger after 3 days would convert some of these losses to breakevens without requiring the trade to move the full trailing stop distance.
**Suggested change:** Test adding a breakeven stop trigger: after 3 trading days, if position is still open and not yet at trailing stop activation level, move stop to entry price. Measure impact on 1-5d win rate and net P&L separately from the 6+d cohort. Note interaction with R2 (see Contradictions).

## Contradictions

### C1: R2 (move position management to iterations 2–4) vs R3 (breakeven trigger at day 3)
**Conflict:** R2 recommends calibrating the trailing stop activation level first (iterations 2–4). R3 recommends testing a day-3 breakeven trigger. If both are tested in iterations 2–4, their effects on the 1–5d cohort will be entangled — the trailing stop activation level changes the distribution of when positions first become profitable, which directly affects whether a day-3 breakeven trigger is safe to apply. Testing them together makes it impossible to isolate which change drives improvement.
**Verdict:** Test trailing stop activation (R2) in iteration 2 in isolation. Only introduce the day-3 breakeven trigger (R3) in iteration 3 after the trailing stop level is fixed. Sequence them; do not combine in the same iteration.

### C2: R1 (reduce universe to 100–150 tickers) vs R7 (per-ticker cooldown rule)
**Conflict:** Reducing the universe to 150 tickers and simultaneously adding a 10-day cooldown per-ticker could severely restrict daily opportunity. If 20% of the 150-ticker universe is in cooldown on a given day, the effective opportunity set drops to ~120 tickers. In low-opportunity market phases, this could result in 0 entries per day.
**Verdict:** Compatible, but the cooldown window should be ≤ 5 trading days, not 10, when paired with a reduced universe. Alternatively, implement R1 first and validate daily entry counts before adding R7.

### C3: R3 (day-3 breakeven trigger) vs R2's finding (1.5 ATR activation already helps)
**Conflict:** The 1.5 ATR activation threshold (from R2) already provides early whipsaw protection by requiring the stock to move 1.5 ATR before the trailing stop follows. A day-3 breakeven trigger adds a *time-based* protection layer. If the stock has not yet moved 1.5 ATR by day 3, both mechanisms are simultaneously trying to protect the position: the trailing stop hasn't activated, and the breakeven stop is now at entry. This double-protection could exit positions that are within normal early-stage volatility.
**Verdict:** No true contradiction — these operate on different axes (price vs. time). However, test with care: measure the day-3 breakeven trigger's effect *only on the subset of trades where trailing stop activation has NOT yet occurred by day 3*. If that subset's win rate is still below 45%, the trigger is warranted.
